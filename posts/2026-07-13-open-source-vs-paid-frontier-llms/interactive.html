The dashed line is the efficient frontier — the most intelligence available at each cost-per-task. Open-weight models sit right on its cheap half: nobody gives you capability for less. What changed in July 2026 is the top-right. Kimi K3 is the first open-weight model to land on the frontier at the high end — index 57.1, third in the world behind only Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol. But read its x-coordinate: at $0.94 per task it costs roughly the same as GPT-5.6 Sol and half of Claude Opus 4.8. Open weights reached the frontier — and started charging frontier prices. Switch to the value axis and the older rule still holds below the ceiling: the cheapest open models return several times more index per task-dollar than anything paid.
